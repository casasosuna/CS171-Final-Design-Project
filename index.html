Sexual assault is a general term for any type of sexual activity that occurs without consent. This could include behavior like unwanted touching or kissing, sexual contact with someone who is under the influence of drugs or alcohol and unable to give an informed "yes" or "no", or rape or attempted rape.

Alexandra isn't sure if she should report her sexual assault.

Sexual assault is one of the least-reported violent crimes in the country — less than 40% of survivors nationwide report it to police.

Alexandra was brave enough to report her sexual assault.

She was one of the just 5% of survivors who reported. But now there's just a 1-in-16 chance that her assailant will get arrested. It's like playing roulette...

Click the wheel to see what happens to Alexandra's assailant. Alexandra was one of the lucky 6%: her assailant was arrested. But even now, there's just a 1-in-10 chance that they'll spend any time in jail... As happens 94% of the time, Alexandra's assailant wasn't arrested. Far too often, assailants don't face arrest because schools downplay the accusations or drop charges entirely.

Unfortunately, this isn't just Alexandra's problem.

Women at top colleges across the country face alarmingly high rates of sexual assault.

Sadly, Alexandra is still at risk for sexual assault even after college.

The rate of sexual assault hasn't fallen nearly as quickly as those of other violent crimes.
